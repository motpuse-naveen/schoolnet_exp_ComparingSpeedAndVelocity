Comparing Speed and Velocity

In this exploriment the object moves forward for 3 seconds at a constant speed and backward for 2 seconds. Suppose it moves backwards at 4 cm/s, its speed is 4 cm/s. Its VELOCITY, however, is -4 cm/s. Velocity has direction, speed does not.

Instructions

1.

Speed v/s Velocity

This exploriment explains the difference between speed and velocity.

Suppose a body moves at a speed of 10 cm/s. Then its speed and velocity are both 10 cm/s. Now suppose it moves backwards at 7 cm/s. Its speed is 7 cm/s but its velocity is -7 cm/s.

Speed is what is called as a scalar - a number. Velocity is what is called a vector - it has a number AND a direction. The velocity is -7 cm/s because the direction is backwards.
